TEST 2Academic Writing

Photosynthesis is a fundamental biological process whereby plants, algae, and certain bacteria convert light energy, predominantly from solar radiation, into chemical energy stored in glucose molecules. This remarkable biochemical transformation occurs primarily within specialized organelles called chloroplasts, which contain the photosynthetic pigment chlorophyll that facilitates light absorption.

The process can be delineated into two distinct phases: the light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions, commonly referred to as the Calvin cycle. The former transpires in the thylakoid membranes and generates ATP and NADPH, while the latter occurs in the stroma and utilizes these energy carriers to synthesize carbohydrates from carbon dioxide.
